 The sphere can be placed on any flat space. In order to properly activate, the sphere needs at least 5 ft of unhindered terrain on each side, or a 10 ft square area. A 10 ft immobile cube of temporal energy springs into existence arond you and the sphere folds out into a workshop and remains stationary for the duration. The spell fails if its area includes a larger creature or more than one creature.  If you or any creature attempts to move, cast a spell or attack through the cubes boundary, the forcefield and workshop vanishes immediately and the spell ends.

\r\n

Upon casting, you can choose or focus on an artisan's tool through your tinker's tools that you are proficient with to be available within the workshop, anyone may use chosen tool for the duration of the spell but only one creature can occupy the workshop at a time. The workshop changes its appearance and the materials within the workshop to suit the needs of the chosen tool. (Ex. if the chosen tool is Smith's Tools, then the workshop may contain a forge, anvil and various equipment.) The workshop has enough materials to maintain gear, and to make simple creations or non-magical items. If someone wished to use the work area for something other than simple creations or gear maintenance, then they must provide the required materials. You may touch another creature proficient with a crafting or artisan's skill, even if you don't have it, channel the proper workshop around the target for its needs.

Creatures within the workstation and proficient in the appropriate artisan's tools have advantage on their crafting skill checks. All tools counts as magical and unbreakable. Equipment and other materials created by this spell dissipate into smoke if moved outside the forcefield.

\r\n

Creatures and objects inside the workshop experience time differently to the outside world.  Upon completing the spell, you choose to either speed up or slow down time inside the workshop by a factor of two. 

\r\n

For example, if time inside the workshop is sped up, a task that would take four hours of normal time to complete can be completed in only two hours of normal time.  If time is slowed down, a spell that usually takes ten minutes of normal time to cast, would instead take twenty minutes of normal time. While a creature works at this bench, they do not need to eat, drink, or sleep, and do not suffer the effects of exhaustion due to lack of sleep. However, they won't gain any of the benefits of a long rest or short rest.

\r\n

Once the spell ends, every tool and material created by the spell fades from existence unless the caster use up the last minutes to cast it again prolonging it for another 2 hours. The spell also ends if its caster moves more than 30 ft from around the workshop.
